## Top 3 Cost Drivers at 1000 MAU
 
1. **LLM output tokens** - $1,892/month = **62.6% of total**. Output tokens dominate because the blended output rate ($5.375/MTok) is 5.8x the input rate. Every extra sentence in the model's response multiplies this directly.
2. **EC2 compute (2x t3.2xlarge)** - $542/month = **17.9% of total**. Compute scales in large discrete steps: one instance is $271/month. At 1000 MAU two are needed; at around 1500 MAU a third would be required.
3. **RDS Multi-AZ** - $199/month = **6.6% of total**. The standby replica costs as much as the primary. At 1000 MAU, dropping to Single-AZ and accepting under 60 seconds of manual failover would cut this to ~$100/month, saving $99/month at the cost of reduced availability.

## Two Cost-Cutting Levers
 
### Lever 1: Route 90% of queries to `claude-3-haiku`
 
At 1000 MAU, switching to 100% Haiku drops token cost from $2,095.5 to:
- Input: 220M x $0.25/M = $55
- Output: 352M x $1.25/M = $440
- Total tokens: $495 vs. $2,095.5 - **saves $1,600/month (53% of the total bill)**
The trade-off is real. Haiku has a 32k context window vs. 200k for Sonnet. For legal document analysis requiring more than ~25,000 tokens of retrieved context, Haiku will silently truncate input. It also produces shorter, less nuanced outputs on complex reasoning tasks - fine for first-draft generation, not for final clause-level analysis.
 
A practical middle ground: automatically route queries with more than 15,000 RAG tokens to Sonnet (roughly 10% of queries), keep the rest on Haiku. That hybrid achieves around 45% savings vs. full Sonnet while preserving quality where it actually matters.
 
### Lever 2: Reserved Instances for Qdrant EC2 + RDS (1-year, no upfront)
 
- r5.large 1-year convertible RI in eu-west-1: ~$0.078/hr = $57/month vs. $92/month On-Demand - **saves $35/month**
- db.r5.large Multi-AZ 1-year RI: ~$0.188/hr = $137/month vs. $199/month - **saves $62/month**
- Total: **$97/month saved (3.2% of the 1000-MAU bill)**
These two resources are stable and won't be resized within a year, so the commitment is low-risk. The no-upfront option means zero cash outlay. The constraint: reduced flexibility if instance types need to change, and a 30-day wait for convertible RI exchanges.
 
**Combined effect at 1000 MAU:** from $3,020/month to roughly **$1,323/month** (-56%), at the cost of Haiku outputs for most queries and a 1-year lock-in on two instance types.

## Unit Economics Summary
 
| MAU | Total cost/mo | Cost per MAU | Marginal cost (next 100 users) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | $621 | $62.13 | ~$21/user |
| 100 | $813 | $8.13 | ~$2.20/user |
| 1000 | $3,020 | $3.02 | ~$2.10/user |
 
**Where does the curve break?** Unit economics fall below $10/user at roughly **85 MAU**. Below that, the fixed infrastructure ($600/month) dominates. Above around 200 MAU, token cost becomes the main driver and marginal cost stabilises around $2-3/user - which is where self-hosting starts to look genuinely attractive vs. SaaS.
 
The key insight is the cost floor: unlike SaaS pricing that scales linearly from user 1, self-hosted has a high fixed cost but a much lower ceiling. At 1000 MAU the self-hosted cost is $3.02/user vs. $60/user for ChatGPT Enterprise - a 95% reduction.

## Pricing Recommendation
 
At **100 MAU**, total cost is $813/month. To cover cost at a 50% gross margin:
 
```
Price per user = ($813 x 1.5) / 100 = $12.20/user/month
```
 
**Recommended price: $12/user/month.**
 
- Gross margin at 100 MAU: 44% (slightly below 50% after rounding down for commercial appeal)
- Gross margin at 500 MAU (estimated cost ~$1,900/month): $12 x 500 = $6,000 revenue - 68% margin
- Gross margin at 1000 MAU: $12 x 1,000 = $12,000 vs. $3,020 cost - **75% margin**
At $12/user/month this sits 80% below ChatGPT Enterprise ($60) and 60% below Microsoft Copilot ($30), while offering data sovereignty, custom MCP tooling, and model flexibility that neither SaaS product provides. The margin projection only holds if the Haiku routing lever gets applied at scale - without it, margins compress significantly above 500 MAU.
